Your roadmap

Follow these steps in order. Each step links to the relevant guide or official authority.

Step-by-step

1
Choose business structure
Freiberufler (freelancer) vs Einzelunternehmen vs UG vs GmbH. For most immigrants starting small: Freiberufler (no registration needed) or Einzelunternehmen (simple trade registration).
→ Before anything else
2
Gewerbeanmeldung (trade registration)
Register your business at the Gewerbeamt of your city. €10–60 fee. Required for Einzelunternehmen and most trades. Freiberufler skip this step.
→ Week 1
3
Register with Finanzamt
Fill in Fragebogen zur steuerlichen Erfassung (tax registration form) at your local Finanzamt. You will receive your Steuernummer within 2–4 weeks.
→ Week 1–2
4
Get a business bank account
Required for separating business and personal finances. Commerzbank, Qonto, Kontist, and N26 Business are popular choices.
→ Week 2
5
Health insurance — self-employed
Self-employed must arrange their own health insurance. Options: public GKV (voluntary, income-based) or private PKV. Apply immediately — no gaps allowed.
→ Week 1
6
Kleinunternehmerregelung decision
If annual revenue <€22,000: you can apply for Kleinunternehmerregelung (no VAT collection required). Decision must be made on your first Finanzamt form.
→ Registration
7
Self-employed visa (§21 AufenthG)
Non-EU citizens need a self-employment residence permit. Requires: business plan, proof of income, relevant qualifications, market need. Apply at Ausländerbehörde.
→ Month 1–3
8
Rentenversicherung (pension)
Some freelancers must register with Deutsche Rentenversicherung. Check if your profession is covered. Voluntary contributions are possible.
→ Month 2
9
File first tax return
Einkommensteuererklärung + Umsatzsteuererklärung (if applicable). Deadline: July 31. Use ELSTER (free) or hire a Steuerberater.
→ Following year
