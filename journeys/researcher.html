Your roadmap

Follow these steps in order. Each step links to the relevant guide or official authority.

Step-by-step

1
Get university hosting agreement
Your German host university/institution must issue a formal Aufnahmevertrag (hosting agreement). Required for the researcher visa.
→ Before applying
2
Apply for researcher visa (§18d AufenthG)
Submit at German embassy with hosting agreement, degree, financial proof, health insurance. Processing: 4–8 weeks.
→ 2–3 months before
3
Degree recognition check
Check Anabin database for recognition status of your degree. Most researcher visas accept foreign PhDs without formal recognition.
→ Before arrival
4
Arrive and register (Anmeldung)
Register at Bürgeramt within 2 weeks. Essential for everything that follows.
→ Week 1
5
Get health insurance
Public GKV via TK/AOK or private PKV. Many research institutions have group plans — check with your host.
→ Week 1
6
Apply for residence permit
Aufenthaltserlaubnis für Forschung at Ausländerbehörde. Bring hosting agreement, Anmeldung, insurance, degree.
→ Month 1
7
Open bank account
DKB or N26 are popular with international researchers. Need IBAN for salary or stipend.
→ Week 1–2
8
Register with university/institution
Immatrikulation if PhD student, or employee registration if postdoc. Get your institutional email and library access.
→ Week 1
9
DFG / DAAD funding check
If not yet funded: apply to DFG (German Research Foundation) or DAAD for fellowships and research grants.
→ Ongoing
10
File tax return (if applicable)
Researchers with salary file Einkommensteuererklärung. Stipend recipients may be tax-exempt — check with host institution.
→ Following year
