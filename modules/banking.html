Money basics

Banking and taxes in Germany

Prepare the right documents, compare real account costs and use official sources for tax questions.

Before opening an account

Banks may ask for identification, a German address or registration evidence, a tax identification number and residence information. Requirements differ by provider.

Compare the full cost

Check monthly fees, card costs, cash withdrawals, transfers, overdraft interest, support languages and cancellation terms.

Protect yourself

Never share a PIN, TAN or one-time login code. Confirm the website domain and contact the bank directly if a message creates urgency.

Step by step

Open and organise your account

  1. Choose the account type. A current account (Girokonto) is commonly used for salary, rent and recurring payments.
  2. Compare providers. Include branch banks, online banks and basic-payment-account options where relevant.
  3. Complete identification. Follow the provider's approved in-person, video or eID process.
  4. Set up payments carefully. Review direct debits and keep enough balance for rent, insurance and utilities.
  5. Keep records. Save contracts, fee schedules, account statements and cancellation confirmations.
