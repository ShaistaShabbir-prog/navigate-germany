📊 Understanding Schufa

Schufa is Germany's credit bureau. A good score (95%+) is needed for renting apartments, getting contracts, and traditional bank accounts.

As a newcomer: You start with a neutral score (not bad, but no history). Build it by:
✅ Opening a German bank account and using it regularly
✅ Registering mobile phone contracts in your name
✅ Paying all bills on time
✅ Getting a credit card (even a secured one)

Free Schufa report: Once per year at meineschufa.de

💶 Monthly financial checklist

📱 Mobile plan: €10-20/month (Aldi Talk, Lidl Connect for budget)
🔒 Haftpflichtversicherung: €5-15/month (ESSENTIAL — liability insurance)
📺 Rundfunkbeitrag (GEZ): €18.36/month (mandatory if you have any internet device)
🌐 Internet: €25-40/month (Vodafone, Telekom, O2)
🚆 Deutschlandticket: €29/month (unlimited local transport across all Germany)
💊 Krankenkasse: employer pays ~50%, you pay ~7.3% of salary
