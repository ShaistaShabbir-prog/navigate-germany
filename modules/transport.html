🎫 Deutschlandticket — €49/month

The Deutschlandticket gives you unlimited travel on all local and regional public transport across Germany for a flat monthly fee.

What it covers

  • ✅ All S-Bahn, U-Bahn, tram, bus (local)
  • ✅ Regional trains (RE, RB, IRE)
  • ✅ Valid across all 16 federal states
  • ✅ No zone restrictions for local transport
  • ❌ NOT valid on ICE, IC, EC trains
  • ❌ NOT valid on long-distance buses
  • ❌ No seat reservation included

How to get it

📱 DB App — bahn.de or DB Navigator app
📱 Local app — MVV, BVG, HVV, KVB etc.
💳 Monthly subscription — cancel anytime
🎓 Student version — often included in Semesterticket
👨‍👩‍👧 Children under 6 — free
🐕 Dogs — need separate ticket usually

💡 Tips for newcomers

  • Subscribe on day 1 in Germany — immediate coverage
  • Download the app before your first day
  • Screenshot your ticket in case of connectivity issues
  • Check if employer subsidises it (many do: Jobticket)
  • Cancel by 10th of month to avoid next month's charge

🚄 Deutsche Bahn — long-distance travel

ICE and IC trains require a separate ticket. Book early for best prices.

BahnCard — worth it?

BahnCard 25
25% discount on all DB tickets. €62.90/year (2nd class). Worth it if you travel ~4× per year.
BahnCard 50
50% discount. €255/year (2nd class). Worth it for frequent travellers (2+ trips/month).

Booking tips

  • Book 6 weeks early — Super Sparpreis from €17.90
  • Sparpreis — fixed train, flexible date ±1 day
  • Flexpreis — any train that day, most expensive
  • DB Navigator app — also shows regional trains
  • Seat reservation — optional but recommended for ICE (€4.90)
  • Delay compensation — claim online if >60 min delay

DB Navigator App

Official Deutsche Bahn app — buy tickets, check live departures, manage bookings.

Claim online if your train was ≥60 minutes late. 25% refund for 60 min, 50% for 120 min.

🚗 Driving licence exchange

Non-EU licences must be exchanged or converted within 6 months of registering in Germany. EU licences are valid without exchange.

Exchange timeline

  • EU/EEA licence — valid indefinitely, no exchange needed
  • Some non-EU countries — direct exchange (no test): USA, UK, Canada, Australia, Japan, Switzerland, and more
  • Other countries — must take German driving test (theory + practical)
  • Apply at — Führerscheinstelle (driver's licence office) in your city
  • Timeline — allow 3–6 months including test waiting times

Required documents

  • Valid passport or EU ID
  • Current driving licence (original)
  • Certified German translation of licence (if not in Latin script)
  • Biometric passport photo (35×45mm)
  • Anmeldebescheinigung (registration confirmation)
  • Sehtest (eye test — from any optician, ~€10)
  • First aid course certificate (Erste Hilfe, 8 hours)

🚲 Bike sharing & e-scooters

Germany has excellent cycling infrastructure. Many immigrants use bikes as their primary transport, especially in flat cities like Berlin, Hamburg, and Cologne.

Nextbike

Germany-wide bike sharing. Docking stations in 100+ cities. From €1/30 min or monthly flat rate.

→ nextbike.de

Deutsche Bahn Call a Bike

DB's bike sharing service integrated into DB Navigator app. Available in major cities.

→ callabike.de

TIER e-scooters

E-scooters in most German cities. Unlock via app. Not usable on footpaths.

→ tier.app

Lime

E-scooters and e-bikes. Available in Berlin, Munich, Hamburg, Cologne.

→ li.me

Buy a used bike

eBay Kleinanzeigen is the best place. Budget €80–200 for a reliable city bike. Always use a good lock (Kryptonite or ABUS).

→ kleinanzeigen.de

⚠ E-scooter rules

  • Must use cycle lanes or road — NOT footpaths
  • No passengers allowed
  • Helmets not mandatory but strongly recommended
  • Not allowed on motorways or in pedestrian zones
  • Must have insurance (usually included in rental price)
